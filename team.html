Team

DANIEL M. ALONZO, M.D.

“All knowledge attains its ethical value and its human significance only by the human sense in which it is employed. Only a good man can be a great physician. ”- Hermann Nothnagel Dr. Daniel Alonzo has been a practicing Otolaryngologist and Head-Neck Surgeon since 1993. He pioneered and led the establishment of the Ear, Nose and Throat department of The Medical City. He was appointed chairman a few years after the ENT department was launched. Dr. Alonzo was also the first ENT specialist to lead a cancer center in the Philippines. He took up his pre-medical degree at the University of Santo Tomas in Sampaloc, Manila. After which, he pursued a medicine degree at the same institution. After completing his otolaryngology residency at the University of Santo Tomas, he further enriched his knowledge and medical training at Osaka University Medical School in Japan for his fellowship in Head and Neck Surgery. At present, Dr. Daniel Alonzo is one of the most well-known Otolaryngologists in the country. He continues to focus to be the best in his field. He continues his quest to improve the lives of his patients by bringing the quality and value back in their lives again.

SAMANTHA CASTAÑEDA, M.D.

“Evidence Based Practice is the conscientious, explicit and judicious use of current best evidence in making decisions about the care of the individual patient. It means integrating individual clinical expertise with the best available external clinical evidence from systematic research.” - Dr. David Sackett Dr. Samantha Soriano-Castañeda has gained the respect and recognition of medical practitioners as one of the very few Head and Neck Reconstructive Surgeons of the country. Annually, she performs reconstructive surgery on almost forty patients; half of which are cancer patients. At present, she works as an Otolaryngologist–Head and Neck Surgeon in The Medical City and Rizal Medical Center, both located in Pasig. Dr. Castaneda obtained her B.S. Psychology degree at the University of the Philippines in Diliman, Quezon City. She then graduated from the University of the Philippines, College of Medicine in Manila. She started her practice in 2000 as an ear, nose and throat doctor after finishing her residency at the Philippine General Hospital. Being a medical practitioner for almost two decades, she keeps her strong stance for evidence-based medicine. She firmly believes that an intensive research and concentrated evidence to a clinical problem can then lead to the most useful evaluation and management. To further hone the expertise of a whole new generation of doctors, Dr. Samantha S. Castañeda also teaches at the Ateneo School of Medicine and Public Health.

PETER SIMON D. JARIN, M.D.

“Help others in their race of life. Remember that when you help another up a mountain you are a little nearer the top yourself.” – Thomas S. Monson Dr. Peter Simon Jarin was a man born to race. He is a triathlete doctor who was always in pursuit of self-development and selfless medical service. He is currently fourteen years in practice in two different hospitals in Metro Manila namely: The Medical City and Quirino Memorial Medical Center. He also provides services in Cainta Municipal Hospital, a government hospital located in the province of Rizal. Dr. Jarin majored in Bachelor of Science in Biology with a minor in Biochemistry at the University of San Francisco. After earning his college degree, he returned to the Philippines to pursue his medicine studies at the University of Santo Tomas. He completed his medical internship and residency program in Otolaryngology - Head and Neck Surgery at the Philippine General Hospital. After which, he then completed his Rhinology fellowship under Professor Yang Gi Min at the Seoul National University Hospital in South Korea. At present, Dr. Jarin’s areas of interest are diseases of the nose and paranasal sinus. His expertise in endoscopic sinus surgery and endoscopic skull base surgeries has benefited patients who encounter grave challenges in health, finances and environment. For that reason, he undertook the commitment to be involved in charitable programs, to provide the highest level of care, to ensure delivery of better outcome in terms of treatment, survival and quality of life.

ESPERANZA ARGELYN ONG, M.D.

“We are physicians. It is a proud title. It carries prerogatives; it carries privileges. Most of all it carries accountability, not only for the future of a great profession but for the very lives of our fellow sufferers from the human condition.” – Lindsey E. Beaton A steadfast health professional can be a prime mover for a greater advocacy that fortifies patient empowerment, comprehensive treatment and exceptional medical services. Such is the life aspiration of a young and driven health provider like Dr. Argelyn Ong. Dr. Ong graduated with a degree in BS Biology from the University of the Philippines, Diliman. After which, she pursued her degree in medicine at the University of Santo Tomas Faculty of Medicine and Surgery. She first exhibited her clinical skills and obtained outstanding mentorship during her residency in Otolaryngology - Head and Neck Surgery at The Medical City. In 2014, she graduated from residency as the “Most Outstanding Trainee” from the same medical institution. She then completed her fellowship training in Head and Neck Surgical Oncology in The Medical City. From the onset of her career, Dr. Ong chose to focus her practice on Head and Neck cases, particularly cancer cases. She collaborates with esteemed specialists who share the same advocacy of giving the highest level of healthcare to all patients.
